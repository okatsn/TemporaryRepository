Stochastic Dynamics and Informational Analysis of Earthquake Rupture Processes

  • Developed and applied stochastic models using the Langevin equation 1 and Fokker-Planck equation 2 to analyze earthquake rupture processes, providing insights into earthquake dynamics and physics-based explanation for empirical scaling laws.
  • Utilized the informational theory 3 approaches to distinguish between different frictional regimes based on both synthetic and experimental data, providing insights into the underlying mechanisms of earthquake process.

  1. Essentially a kind of stochastic differential equation (SDE) that describes the evolution of a system under both deterministic and random forces.

  2. A kind of partial differential equation (PDE) governing the time evolution of the probability density function of a stochastic variable, that shares a similar diffusion-like form with the Shallow-Ice Approximation (SIA) equation, but describes the evolution of probability densities of rupture momentum rather than ice thickness.

  3. Fisher-Shannon analysis

Langevin equation
  • Straightforward physics reasoning
  • Rupture processes realized as “sample paths”
Fokker-Planck equation
  • Describe the same process from the perspective of “probability density”

\[ \frac{\partial P(v,t)}{\partial t} = - \frac{\partial}{\partial v} \Bigg( \left( - F_C \frac{v(t)}{|v(t)|} + F_\text{ext} \right) P(v,t) \Bigg) \\ + D\frac{\partial^2}{\partial v^2}P(v,t) \]

Machine Learning for Soil Water Content Estimation

  • Utilized Classification and Regression Tree (CART) algorithm to estimate soil water content (SWC) using meteorological inputs for agricultural purposes in Taiwan.
  • Employed advanced feature selection and time-series cross-validation techniques to analyze the responsiveness of SWC at different soil depths to various meteorological parameters, providing a detailed analysis of depth-dependent time delays and saturation effects.
  • Contributed to the understanding of seasonal hydrodynamic variations in response to meteorological changes, offering critical insights that can enhance agricultural water management practices.

Machine Learning for Soil Water Content Estimation

  • Supported by National Science and Technology Council (Taiwan), this project produced one published paper, one Julia package and a technical report over two years.

Advanced Techniques in Geo-Electromagnetic Earthquake Precursor Detection

  • Integrated GEMSTIP and MagTIP 1 algorithms for analyzing earthquake-related geo-electromagnetic anomalies.
  • Conducted a comprehensive analysis of 12 years of geo-electromagnetic data, identifying key statistical indicators and their relationships to earthquake events.

  1. GeoElectric Monitoring System (GEMS) with extended adaptability to geomagnetic data, based on Time of Increased Probability (TIP) concept. For more details please refer to “Testing the correlations between anomalies of statistical indexes of the geoelectric system and earthquakes” (H.-J Chen & C.-C Chen, 2016).
